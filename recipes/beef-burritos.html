Cheesy Beef Breakfast Burritos

Instructions

In a pan over medium to medium-high heat, spray olive oil spray and add 280 grams (10 ounces) 96/4 lean ground beef. When beef is about 70 percent cooked, add in 15 grams (3 tsp) Old El Paso 25% less sodium taco seasoning, stir into the meat, and cook the meat until fully browned.

Set meat aside, turn heat down to in between low- medium and medium heat and add 230 grams (~1 cup) egg beaters. Rinse 70 grams (~2/2 cup fat free shredded cheddar cheese and add the cheese to the eggs. When eggs are almost fully cooked, add a dash of salt and pepper to taste to the eggs and finish cooking.

Lay a tortilla down and top with 1/5 of the meat, 1/5 of the eggs, 26 grams Tostito’s salsa con queso, and fold into a burrito.

To make the chipotle sauce for dipping, mix 150 grams (~2/3) cup plain non-fat Greek yogurt, 10 grams (~1 tbsp) sriracha, and 5 grams (1.5 tsp) Hidden Valley ranch seasoning.

To store the burritos, wrap each burrito in tin foil and you can store in the fridge for up to 5 days or the freezer for up to a month. Burritos may be able to last more than a month in the freezer but I personally don’t keep them longer than that. To store the sauce, place in an air tight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Again, the sauce may last longer, I just personally don’t keep it for more than 5 days.
